Testicular Cancer

Normal Reference Range Table
Learning Objectives
  • 1. Describe the effects of cryptorchidism and testicular torsion.
  • 2. List the most common pathogen causing epididymitis in men younger than 35 years and in men older than 35 years.
  • 3. Compare and contrast the morphologic and clinical features of the two major types of testicular germ cell tumors: seminoma and nonseminomatous germ cell tumors. Identify the most common testicular tumor in children.
  • 4. Describe how the serum markers human chorionic gonadotropin (HCG) and alpha-fetoprotein (AFP) are related to germ cell tumors of the testis.
  • 5. Identify the sexually transmitted disease associated with the development of penile squamous cell carcinoma.

Scenario

A 35-year-old healthy man with a past history of cryptorchidism repaired at age 5 presented with painless enlargement of the left testis. The mass was opaque on transillumination. A testicular ultrasound examination revealed the enlargement to be composed of soft tissue without a cystic component. Laboratory data included serum human chorionic gonadotropin (HCG) of 90 mU/mL (reference range <5 mU/mL) and alpha-fetoprotein (AFP) of 7 ng/mL (reference range 0-44 ng/mL). A radical left orchiectomy was performed. The left testicle was dominated by a 4-cm, pink-tan nodular mass (Images 1-3). An abdominal CT scan revealed para-aortic lymphadenopathy; a chest x-ray was normal. Radiotherapy was administered to the abdominal retroperitoneal region.

Image 1
Tumor
Spermatic cord
Residual testicular parenchyma

Testicle, classic seminoma - Gross, cut surface

The specimen has been bisected. Note the bulging, nodular, tan tumor replacing most of the gonad. The tumor has a fairly uniform appearance and lacks cysts, necrosis, or obvious hemorrhage. This is the typical gross appearance of a pure seminoma.

Question 1
1
How is this different from the usual gross appearance of a nonseminomatous germ cell tumor?
Show answer

Nonseminomatous germ cell tumors usually have a heterogeneous gross appearance with areas of necrosis, hemorrhage, and, often, cyst formation.


Question 2
2
What is an important risk factor for testicular tumors?
Show answer

Cryptorchidism is an important risk factor for testicular tumors.


Image 2
Tumor cells
Fibrous trabeculae with lymphocytes

Testis, classic seminoma - Low power

Classic seminomas are composed of sheets of uniform polygonal cells with clear cytoplasm, round nuclei, and prominent nucleoli. Typically, the cells are divided into ill-defined nodules by fibrous trabeculae containing lymphocytes


Question 1
1
What is the prognosis for classic seminomas confined to the testicle?
Show answer

The prognosis is excellent.


Question 2
2
What is the prognosis for metastatic classic seminoma?
Show answer

More than 95% of patients with tumors confined to the testicle or metastatic to the subdiaphragmatic lymph nodes are cured.


Image 3
Seminoma cells
Fibrous trabeculae with lymphocytes
Mitotic figure

Testis, classic seminoma - High power

The clear cytoplasm, round nucleus, and prominent nucleoli of the seminoma cells are best seen at high power. Note the fibrous trabeculae infiltrated with lymphocytes.

Question 1
1
In addition to surgery, is the primary mode of therapy for pure classic seminomas chemotherapy or radiotherapy?
Show answer

The primary mode of therapy is radiotherapy because seminomas are very radiosensitive.


Question 2
2
Are seminomas associated with increases in serum levels of human chorionic gonadotropin (HCG) or AFP?
Show answer

Seminomas are not associated with an increase in AFP levels. Approximately 10% to 15% of seminomas contain syncytial giant cells that contain HCG. In these patients, serum HCG is moderately elevated. This feature does not affect treatment or prognosis.


Image 4
Germ cell tumor
Chondroid component

Testis, nonseminomatous (mixed) germ cell tumor - Gross, cut surface

The typical appearance of a mixed germ cell tumor with various textures and colors is shown.

Question 1
1
Note the gray, glassy, chondroid region; this chondroid element is a portion of what type of germ cell tumor?
Show answer

This chondroid element is part of a teratoma; teratomas can have tissues derived from all three germ cell layers.


Image 5
Gland-like structures

Testis, embryonal carcinoma - Low power

Compared with classic seminoma, embryonal carcinoma is composed of more pleomorphic cells, which often are arranged into trabeculae or gland-like formations.

Question 1
1
Nonseminomatous germ cell tumors have often metastasized by the time they are discovered; after orchiectomy, is the primary mode of therapy chemotherapy or radiotherapy?
Show answer

Chemotherapy is the primary mode of therapy.


Image 6
Synctiotrophoblasts
Choriocarcinoma

Testis, embryonal carcinoma with syncytiotrophoblasts - Medium power

Syncytiotrophoblasts may be seen in all germ cell tumors, including classic seminomas; however, a diagnosis of choriocarcinoma requires syncytiotrophoblasts and cytotrophoblasts.

Question 1
1
What serum marker may be present in germ cell tumors with syncytiotrophoblasts?
Show answer

HCG may be present.


Image 7
Schiller-Duvall body

Testis, yolk sac tumor (endodermal sinus tumor) - High power

Yolk sac tumors may display several architectural patterns, including microcysts and trabeculae, as well as perivascular pseudorosettes termed Schiller-Duvall bodies.

Question 1
1
Which serum marker is often elevated with yolk sac tumor?
Show answer

AFP is often elevated with yolk sac tumor.


Question 2
2
What histologic feature is commonly seen in yolk sac tumors?
Show answer

Hemorrhage is commonly seen in yolk sac tumors.


Question 3
3
What is the most common testicular tumor of infants and children up to 3 years of age?
Show answer

Yolk sac tumor is the most common testicular tumor of infants and children up to 3 years of age. This tumor has an excellent prognosis.


Image 8
Infarcted, hemorrhagic testicle

Testicle, torsion - Gross

The normal testicular parenchyma is tan. This testicle is swollen and dark red to black. A microscopic section would reveal hemorrhagic necrosis.

Question 1
1
What symptom is associated with this lesion?
Show answer

Sudden severe testicular pain is an associated symptom.


Image 9
Ulcerated tumor
Retracted foreskin

Penis, squamous cell carcinoma - Gross

The foreskin has been retracted to reveal this ulcerating squamous cell carcinoma at the edge of the glans.

Question 1
1
What group of lymph nodes (location) is typically the first involved by metastatic penile squamous cell carcinoma?
Show answer

The inguinal lymph nodes typically are first.


Image 10
Squamous eddies

Penis, invasive squamous cell cancer - Low power

Penile carcinomas are usually squamous cell carcinomas and often display squamous eddies.

Question 1
1
Which sexually transmitted disease is associated with the development of penile squamous cell carcinoma?
Show answer

Human papillomavirus infection is associated with the development of penile squamous cell carcinoma.
